OVERVIEW




In the past years the media has been, and still continuously being stripped off of their freedom, journalists were killed and some media outlets were shut down. Free press is a fundamental aspect of democracy, allowing the public access to diverse information and fostering transparency, but due to this unjust system, our website aims to give a tribute to those journalists for their bravery. Which we believe could be an inspiration to our aspiring future journalists.


Attack on Press: Breaking Chains, Sharing Truths contains news, articles, and stories about the struggles and issues that journalists are currently facing in the country. The website mainly talks about Press Freedom; which holds the people to publish information and their expressions without the fear and without the hindrance of facing threats, harassments, and red-tagging.





Journalist Gallery

A short tribute and a memory lane to some of the journalists that showed their bravery, dedication in every news, and their firm stand to tell the truth will not be forgotten.

Rex Cornelio Pepino

“Behind the killing of Rex Cornelio was to silence him” - Joel Sy Engco, task force’s executive director. On May 05, 2020, Cornelio was shot dead by unidentified motor rider assailants while he and his wife were on their way home. Cornelio was known as an environmental advocate and reporting cases of local corruption.

Ronnie Villamor

On November 14, 2020, a Masbate freelance journalist was shot dead by the soldiers in the province of Milagros. His death was seen by CPJ and it caused them to update their annual ranking wherein journalists are slain and their killers are free. Villamor was known for his talks about the political issues for Dos Kantos Balita. It covers social issues like Illegal logging, drug trafficking, and illegal fishing in their town.

Virgillo Maganes

“I condemn the killing of Virgilio Maganes in the Philippines. An attack against a journalist is an attack against the whole of society’s right to freedom of expression” - Audrey Azoulay, UNESCO Director-General. Maganes, was a commentator on DWPR radio station, also a columnist for the weekly Northern Watch newspaper that covered issues about politics. On November 10, 2020 he was shot by two unidentified gunmen. It was reported that he was shot 6 times and died on the scene.

Jesus "JESS" Malabanan

Jesus "Jess" Malabanan, a correspondent of Manila Standard Today and the longtime stringer of British news organization Reuters, was shot and killed in his own vicinity in Calbayog, Samar. Malabanan has contributed reporting about the former President Duterte's War on Drugs and won the Pulitzer Prize 2018.

Percival "Percy Lapid" Mabasa

“If he wanted to call out someone, he would do that. If he wanted to say someone was breaking the law, he would do that.” - Jonathan de Santos, chair of the National Union of Journalists of the Philippines (NUJP). Percival Lapid was shot dead on his way home on October 03, 2022. The famous radio commentator was known for his passion, brave commentaries, and his criticism of the past and present administration.

Jhannah Villegas

“The killings occurred could be a work-related motive.” - Jonathan De Santos, chair of National Union of Journalists of the Philippines (NUJP). Jhannah Villegas was known for her news reports about local politics and her criticizing people and she also had an exposure to an online news blog that covers local political events. Her bravery comes to an end, she was shot dead at her home, on April 15, 2022 by the unidentified assailants. Her husband said that the journalist received an anonymous death threat the night before she died.

Cresenciano Cris Bundoquin

“The heinous killing of journalist Cris Bundoquin in cold blood poses an unacceptable violation to press freedom and freedom of expression.” - IFJ General Secretary Anthony Bellanger. Cresenciano Cris Bundoquin, a radio broadcaster was shot to death by two motorcycle-riding assailants. He hosted the Ayos Mandin radio program on the local broadcast station DWXR 101.7 Kalahi FM. Unesco said that Bundoquin reported several local issues and he had received death threats that related to his news reports.

Juan Jumalon

“The incident shows the utter disregard for human life and the rule of law by the perpetrator.” - Kapisanan ng mga Brodkaster ng Pilipinas (KBP). It is associated with the brutalness killing of a radio anchor, Juan Jumalon or known as “DJ Johnny Walker” that happened on November 05, 2023. He was gunned down and stole his gold necklace while broadcasting a live show on Facebook. His program discusses their issues that are circulating within their neighborhood.



Effects

Attacking journalists has far-reaching consequences, not only for individuals involved but for the society as a whole. The landscape of media has been enormously invaded by fear due to several events, where some journalists are being harassed and the potential risk might put them in an unfavorable situation. Given that journalists play a vital role in holding governments and institutions accountable, their determination to disseminate awareness for each individual has been out of bound. With that, continuous attacks emerge and make a huge effect on the media, discouraging journalists from pursuing investigative reporting or covering critical issues. Moreover, execution of journalists contributes to a decline in public trust in media outlets and institutions, as they serve as the key instrument in the area of press media.


Besides, persistent attacks can lead to diplomatic consequences and strain relationships with other countries. In our society, especially nowadays, issues such as corruption, human rights abuses, and social injustices might go unreported if attacks continue, hindering positive change. Eventually, lack of diverse perspectives limits public discourse, interfering societal understanding. The impact of attacking journalists goes beyond individual cases; as it shapes the landscape of information, governance, and societal progress. Keeping safe of journalist's integrity safe is not just a matter of protecting a profession; it is about preserving the very essence of democracy and human rights.
